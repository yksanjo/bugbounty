## 🎓 Best for Complete Beginners

### Start Here (VDPs - No Bounty, Good Practice):
1. **U.S. Department of Defense** (HackerOne) - Great for learning
2. **U.S. Agency for International Development** (HackerOne)
3. **Grindr** (Bugcrowd) - Simple attack surface
4. **Clubhouse** (HackerOne) - Smaller surface, easier bugs

### Move to Paid Programs After 2-3 VDP Submissions:
1. **Opera** (Bugcrowd) - Beginner-friendly, good triage
2. **Automattic** (HackerOne) - Clear scope, responsive team
3. **GitLab** (HackerOne) - Open source, well-documented

## 💡 Strategy for First Bounty

### Week 1-2: Learn & Practice
- Practice on DVWA or Juice Shop
- Read 50+ disclosed HackerOne reports
- Learn OWASP Top 10

### Week 3-4: VDP Practice
- Submit 2-3 valid bugs to VDPs
- Learn how to write good reports
- Build reputation on platform

### Week 5-6: Paid Programs
- Pick ONE program
- Go deep (not wide)
- Focus on 1 vulnerability type

### Week 7+: First Bounty!
- Expect rejection/duplicate at first
- Keep improving
- First bounty usually comes after 20-40 hours of focused testing

---

## 🔥 Quick Wins (Easiest Bugs for Beginners)

1. **Missing Security Headers** - Rarely pays, but good learning
2. **Open Redirect** - Check redirect parameters
3. **Information Disclosure** - Look for exposed files/configs
4. **IDOR** - Change IDs in API calls
5. **Reflected XSS** - Test search boxes and forms
